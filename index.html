Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

I conidered these roles through my choices in visuals, layout, and branding to represent my identity. Technology played a major role through tools like the Adobe suite. Ethically, I considered how accurately and responsibly I was representing myself, especially in my personal branding and when responding to feedback in the UX testing project, making sure my designs were inclusive, clear, and not misleading.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

I applied many of the design principles in my first set of projects. I got to really dive deep into them with the Good Design project, in which I was able to identify many of the concepts that made a successful designp

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I got a lot of useful feedback within the classroom environment. For instance, getting to give and receive advice on my Logos from actual designers helped me revise based on more than the answers given by my interviewees.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

I was able to work a lot on revisions after I created my logos. After my interviews, I went back and was able to further revise my logo so I could choose the best one and improve upon it.

5. Learn to present your work.

I was nervous presenting my first ever project in class, but being able to introduce myself to my peers and discuss lighter topics helped prepare me to present the rest of my actual designs.
